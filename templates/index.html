Who we are

We are a group of people excited to explore the intersection between the hardware we augment our lives with
and the software that drives it, and we see IoT software as an opportunity for innovation.

We organize a meetup in San Francisco to share ideas, projects and experiences.
We offer a space to hack on affordable hardware like Raspberry Pi, Arduino, LightBlue Bean to name a few.

Hack on software for Internet of Things devices and build fun stuff.

In this meetup, we are excited to explore the intersection between the hardware that augments our lives and the software that drives it. This meetup is focused on hands-on hacking!

Join if you want to:

  • Learn about and hack on the hardware and open source software that is defining the IoT space today
  • Help create a diverse, inclusive and supportive community focused on learning and having fun
  • Leverage the possibilities of IoT to benefit others

  • Check us out at meetup.com

Projects to get inspired

This is not a collection of projects we found on the web and looked cool, we actually built these.

Remote lamp switch

With a Raspberry Pi as the main hub control a lamp switch connected to an Arduino. The switch is exposed through a webserver so you can turn your lamp on/off from your phone!

Graphing PIR Motion sensor data

With Electric Imp and Keen IO, record and graph movements around your house.
